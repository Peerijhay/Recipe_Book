How to Prepare Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian soup made with melon seeds, leafy greens, and protein. It is a hearty and flavorful soup that is perfect for a cold day.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi seeds
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped haba単ero pepper (optional)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups chopped leafy greens, such as spinach or bitter leaf
  • 1 pound cooked meat or fish, such as beef, chicken, or shrimp (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, soak the egusi seeds in water for 30 minutes.
  2. While the egusi seeds are soaking, heat the palm oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  3. Add the onion, bell pepper, and haba単ero pepper (if using) to the pot and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Drain the egusi seeds and add them to the pot. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring constantly.
  5. Add the beef broth, chicken broth, diced tomatoes, crayfish, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 30 minutes, or until the egusi seeds have softened.
  6. Stir in the leafy greens and cooked meat or fish (if using) and cook until the greens are wilted, about 5 minutes.
  7. Serve immediately with rice or your favorite side dish.

Tips

* For a thicker soup, you can mash some of the egusi seeds in a mortar and pestle before adding them to the pot. * If you don't have haba単ero peppers, you can use another type of chili pepper, such as jalape単o or cayenne peppers. * You can add other vegetables to the soup, such as carrots, celery, or potatoes. * To make a vegetarian version of the soup, omit the meat or fish and add an extra cup of leafy greens.

Enjoy!
